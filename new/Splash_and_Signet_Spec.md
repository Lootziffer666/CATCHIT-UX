# Splash_and_Signet_Spec

## Purpose
Defines the CatchIt signet specification and splash animation specification.

## Status
Working reference spec for CatchIt branding motion.

## Scope
- signet character
- point, opening, and stroke rules
- signet-only vs wordmark combination
- splash sequence
- timing
- first launch vs repeat launch
- motion rules
- motion no-gos

## Working rule
This spec defines the current intended behavior of the CatchIt signet and splash sequence.
It should preserve the product’s tone:
- poetic-functional
- calm
- guided
- human
- condensed
and avoid drift into:
- techy loader behavior
- childish animation
- generic app intro spectacle

---

# 1. Signet Spec

## 1.1 Core Meaning
The CatchIt signet is not a perfect orbit and not a neutral loading ring.

It is:
- a guided line
- an enclosed movement
- a held path
- condensed motion turned into orientation

It should feel:
- human
- caught
- stabilized
- quietly alive

It should not feel:
- mathematical
- decorative-chaotic
- comic
- sterile
- like a spinner

---

## 1.2 Circle Character

### Target Character
The circle is:
- almost circular
- slightly irregular
- quietly guided
- subtly route-like

### Form Rules
- base form: approximately 85–90% circular
- deviation: approximately 10–15% controlled irregularity
- allow 4–5 subtle directional shifts
- no hard corners
- no visible intersections
- no chaotic bumps
- no brush texture as the main characteristic

### Forbidden
- perfect geometric circle
- expressive brushstroke ring
- overt map-diagram look
- too many waves or kinks
- messy scribble quality

### Principle
Not perfect, but guided.

---

## 1.3 Point

### Character
The point is:
- clear
- calm
- more precise than the line
- not playful

### Rules
- the point remains cleaner than the circle
- the point acts as the visual fixpoint
- the point must not feel as rough or alive as the line
- point size should be roughly 1.4–1.8x the circle stroke thickness as a starting ratio
- the point must not dominate the signet

### Principle
The line lives, the point holds.

---

## 1.4 Point Distance

### Core Rule
The point sits close to the opening, but does not touch it.

### Target Effect
It should feel:
- related
- intentional
- positioned
- tensioned

It should not feel:
- glued on
- detached
- accidental

### Practical Rule
Distance between the line ending and the point:
- approximately 0.6–1.1x point diameter

### Principle
Connected, not touching.

---

## 1.5 Opening

### Purpose
The opening is intentional.
It is not a broken circle.
It is a held relation to the point.

### Rules
- the opening sits on the right side near the point
- upper and lower line endings should feel intentionally oriented toward the point
- the opening must neither collapse nor become too wide
- it should create controlled incompleteness

### Recommended Range
Opening angle:
- approximately 18°–28°

### Forbidden
- almost closed circle with no readable opening
- very large gap
- arbitrary hole
- line endings that do not relate to the point

### Principle
Not closed. Not open. Held.

---

## 1.6 Stroke Thickness

### Goal
The circle must remain:
- readable
- stable
- quiet
- non-technical

### Rules
- stroke should be even enough to remain a sign
- minimal natural variance is allowed
- do not use expressive brush-width fluctuations
- do not use a hairline-thin vector stroke
- in small sizes, prefer slightly more robustness over refinement loss

### Forbidden
- texture-led brushstroke
- dramatic thickness changes
- sterile vector perfection

### Principle
Line before texture.

---

## 1.7 Signet-only

### Valid Use Cases
Signet-only is valid for:
- splash end state
- app icon
- loader-like brand presence
- small brand markers
- micro-branding
- reduced states

### Rules
- signet-only must remain legible without the wordmark
- opening and point relationship must still carry the form
- signet-only must never behave like a generic loading spinner
- signet-only must always be treated as branding, not utility UI

### Principle
Legible alone, not only as companion form.

---

## 1.8 Wordmark Combination

### Valid Use Cases
Wordmark + signet is valid for:
- splash
- brand screen
- intro
- larger hero moments
- communicative surfaces

### Rules
- the wordmark sits within the signet’s visual field
- the circle must not overpower the wordmark
- the wordmark must remain readable during reveal
- the point must not collide visually with the last letter
- the signet does not mechanically frame text; it holds the name in motion

### Principle
Not a frame around text, but movement around the name.

---

## 1.9 Final Signet Target
The signet should feel like:
- a caught movement
- a held route
- a quiet path
- condensed orientation

It should not feel like:
- a loader
- a planet/orbit symbol
- a brush experiment
- a route planner icon
- a decorative doodle

---

# 2. Splash Sequence Spec

## 2.1 Purpose
The splash is not decorative branding theater.
It expresses CatchIt’s product logic in miniature:

- movement appears
- movement is caught
- something becomes visible
- motion condenses
- calm remains
- the app opens

---

## 2.2 Core Dramaturgy
Chaos → Catching → Revealing → Condensing → Quiet → Transition

---

## 2.3 Shot Sequence

## Shot 1 — Background Already Present
### Content
- cloud sky background is already visible
- minimal depth and minimal calm movement
- no logo yet

### Duration
- approximately 0.25–0.40 s

### Purpose
- establish world immediately
- avoid hard black-to-logo startup feeling

---

## Shot 2 — Motion Trails Appear
### Content
- a small number of light motion trails appear
- ideally 3–5 relevant trails
- they feel like paths or drifting movement, not action swooshes

### Duration
- approximately 0.40–0.70 s

### Purpose
- introduce raw movement

---

## Shot 3 — Nets Catch and Wipe Clear
### Content
- nets enter the frame
- they catch the motion trails as tools, not as comic characters
- through their sweeping motion, the CatchIt wordmark is revealed

### Duration
- approximately 0.70–1.30 s

### Purpose
- reveal instead of fade-in

### Important
- nets must not cover the wordmark for too long
- the reveal must stay legible
- the nets must not feel cute or character-like

---

## Shot 4 — Wordmark Briefly Stands
### Content
- CatchIt wordmark is readable
- brief moment of calm
- no major secondary motion

### Duration
- approximately 0.20–0.35 s

### Purpose
- recognizability
- brief brand registration

---

## Shot 5 — Point Appears, Signet Writes Itself
### Content
- point appears
- it does not travel in a perfect circle
- it traces a guided, alive path
- the hand-drawn signet circle forms around the wordmark

### Duration
- approximately 0.45–0.70 s

### Purpose
- condense motion into brand form

### Important
- this must feel drawn or written, not loaded
- avoid spinner behavior completely

---

## Shot 6 — Wordmark Dissolves, Signet Remains
### Content
- wordmark softly disappears
- signet remains alone for a short moment

### Duration
- approximately 0.20–0.35 s

### Purpose
- essence reduction
- establish signet as independent brand carrier

---

## Shot 7 — Transition into App
### Content
- the splash wipes or lifts upward
- main screen or first-launch tutorial is revealed underneath

### Duration
- approximately 0.35–0.55 s

### Purpose
- open into the functional product
- avoid hard scene cut

---

## 2.4 Total Duration

### Repeat Launch
- approximately 2.4–3.3 s

### First Launch
- approximately 3.2–4.2 s

### Rule
Do not go significantly longer.
Otherwise it drifts into:
- spectacle
- studio-intro feeling
- unnecessary branding theater

---

## 2.5 First Launch vs Repeat Launch

## First Launch
May tell slightly more:
- motion trails slightly clearer
- wipe reveal slightly more legible
- signet formation slightly more readable
- a little more calm between key phases

### Goal
World, tone, first impression.

---

## Repeat Launch
Must be tighter:
- fewer motion trails
- shorter net presence
- faster wordmark read
- faster signet formation
- quicker transition into app

### Goal
Recognition, not show.

---

## 2.6 Motion Rules

### Allowed
- soft guided motion
- slightly imperfect line formation
- calm wipe/reveal behavior
- subtle background depth motion
- brief quiet pause before app transition

### Not Allowed
- bounce
- exaggerated easing theatrics
- hectic particles
- loader spin
- slapstick chase energy
- comic net animation
- aggressive glow effects
- spinner-like orbiting

### Principle
Not playful, but caught.

---

## 2.7 Nets Style Rules

### Allowed
- sketch-like
- lightly surreal
- poetic
- readable as tools

### Not Allowed
- cute
- comic
- childish
- personified
- overly detailed
- physically heavy or realistic in a literal way

### Principle
Tool, not character.

---

## 2.8 Signet Build Rules in Motion

### Point Movement
- short
- precise
- calm
- never frantic

### Circle Formation
- feels drawn
- not loaded
- not mathematically orbiting
- not brushy or painterly

### Wordmark
- is revealed
- not simply hard-faded in

---

## 2.9 App Transition

### Rule
The transition into the product must not feel like a hard cut.

It should feel like:
- opening
- continuing
- releasing the app beneath

### Form
- splash wipes/lifts upward
- main screen or tutorial is revealed below
- no sudden zoom
- no hard fade-to-app
- no bottom-sheet-style emergence

---

## 2.10 Motion No-Gos

### Hard No-Gos
- generic loader look
- perfect clean orbit
- too many motion trails
- nets staying too long on screen
- childish or cute animation energy
- app-store motivational tone
- too much logo showtime
- frantic point behavior
- loud signet formation
- bottom-sheet-feeling app transition

---

# 3. Compact Master Rules

## Signet
Almost circle, slight route feel, quiet imperfection, clean point.

## Splash
Movement is caught, revealed, condensed, calmed, and opened upward into the app.

## Tone
Poetic-functional, not playful, not techy, not childish.
